# Writing Guidelines

Standards for all copy on digitaljesse.com. Follow these to create content that sounds human, converts visitors, and positions Jesse as a strong job candidate.

---

## Core Principles

### 1. Results First
Lead with outcomes, not responsibilities. Hiring managers want to know what you've accomplished.

**Bad:** "Managed SEO for an e-commerce website"
**Good:** "Grew organic traffic 340% in 6 months for a Shopify art store"

### 2. Specific Beats Vague
Numbers, percentages, and concrete examples are more convincing than adjectives.

**Bad:** "Significant revenue growth"
**Good:** "$2M+ in online sales across multiple brands"

### 3. Human Over Corporate
Write like you talk. Contractions are fine. Personality is good. Stiff corporate speak is not.

**Bad:** "I leverage data-driven strategies to optimize digital presence"
**Good:** "I dig into analytics to figure out what's actually working"

---

## Voice & Tone

### Do
- Be confident without bragging
- Use first person ("I built", "I grew")
- Include occasional casual language
- Vary sentence length
- Get to the point quickly

### Don't
- Use buzzwords or jargon
- Write in third person
- Sound like a press release
- Pad sentences with filler
- Use clichÃ©s

|-----------|--------------|

## Banned Phrases

| Never Use | Use Instead |
|-----------|-------------|
| "I'm passionate about..." | Just show the work |
| "Results-driven professional" | Show actual results |
| "Think outside the box" | Give a real example |
| "Wear many hats" | List actual skills |
| "Team player" | Show collaboration examples |
| "Detail-oriented" | Prove it with work |
| "In today's fast-paced world..." | Delete, start with value |
| "Whether you're looking for..." | Delete, be direct |
| "Take your X to the next level" | Describe the actual outcome |
| "Craft compelling narratives" | Just say "write" |

## Page-Specific Guidelines

### Homepage

**Goal:** Hook visitors in 5 seconds. Answer "Who is this and why should I care?"

**Structure:**
1. Clear headline (who you are + what you do)
2. One-sentence value prop
3. Key achievement or proof point
4. Clear CTA

**Example:**
> **Jesse Johnson**
> Digital marketer and e-commerce specialist with $2M+ in online sales.
> I build brands that grow. Check out my work.

**Don't:**
- Start with "Welcome to my portfolio"
- Use vague descriptions ("digital enthusiast")
- Bury the good stuff below the fold

---

### About Page

**Goal:** Build credibility and connection. Let them know the person behind the work.

**Include:**
- Your story (brief, relevant)
- Key achievements with numbers
- Skills tied to results
- What you're looking for now

**Tone:** Professional but personable. You're a real person, not a LinkedIn bot.

**Don't:**
- Write a novel
- List every job you've ever had
- Use third person
- Sound desperate

---

### Portfolio/Case Studies

**Goal:** Prove you can do the work. Show process and results.

**Structure for each project:**
1. Project name and one-line description
2. The challenge/goal
3. What you did (brief)
4. Results (numbers preferred)
5. Visuals (screenshots, metrics)

**Example:**
> **Luxury Wall Art**
> Built and scaled a Shopify + Etsy art store from zero to $120K in revenue.
>
> **Challenge:** Create a profitable e-commerce business in a crowded market.
>
> **What I did:** Product sourcing via AI tools, SEO strategy (340% organic growth), paid ads management, content creation.
>
> **Results:** 3,000+ products, 4,900+ ranking keywords, profitable within 12 months.

---

### Contact Page

**Goal:** Make it easy to reach you. Remove friction.

**Include:**
- Simple form (name, email, message)
- Direct email address
- LinkedIn link
- What you're looking for ("open to full-time roles in digital marketing")

**Don't:**
- Ask for too much info
- Hide your email
- Use vague CTAs ("reach out")

## SEO Guidelines

### Meta Titles
- 50-60 characters max
- Include your name
- Be specific about what the page offers

**Examples:**
- "Jesse Johnson | Digital Marketing Portfolio"
- "About Jesse Johnson | E-commerce & SEO Specialist"
- "Portfolio | Luxury Wall Art Case Study"

### Meta Descriptions
- 140-160 characters
- Include a key achievement or differentiator
- End with implied CTA or value

**Examples:**
- "Jesse Johnson is a digital marketer with $2M+ in online sales. View portfolio and case studies from e-commerce, SEO, and brand projects."
- "See how Jesse grew Luxury Wall Art to $500K+ revenue through SEO and e-commerce strategy. Full case study with metrics."

## Quick Checklist

Before publishing any copy, verify:

- [ ] No banned words or phrases
- [ ] At least one specific number or result
- [ ] Sentences vary in length
- [ ] Uses contractions naturally
- [ ] Answers "so what?" for the reader
- [ ] Sounds like a human wrote it
- [ ] Gets to the point in the first sentence
- [ ] No em dashes (use commas or periods)
